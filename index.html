what i do

Researcher

Since 2022, I've been fortunate enough to be working as an independent, undergraduate Researcher in the Philanthropics Informatics Research Lab (PIRL) with the guidance of my supervisor Amy Voida (PhD.) at the University of Colorado Boulder. My current research work focuses on the ways that labor unions leverage data and information technologies to support labor power and autonomy.

Information
Scientist

Solving problems with people and data is my speciality. As an Information Scientist, my studies are focused on bridging the gaps between people using technology is what I aim to do. I specialize in making insights from datasets accessible and interactive, using tools like Tableau, altair, and R. Currently, I'm working on learning about using the logistic regression for classification.

Programmer

While serving in a volunteer role as Treasurer for a local non-profit, I found an opportunity for making information even more accessible to community members: Automated and Interactive Data visualizations. So I began to teach myself how to program in Python and now, making technology work for people is my passion. I work on it as often as I can. At the moment, I'm working on learning about asynchronous programming in Python.
